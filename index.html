What Is Learner Experience Design?

Learner Experience Design (LXD) combines the creativity of design with the field of training and development, with aspects of game design, cognitive psychology, and education. In practical terms, that means creating digital experiences that are based on how people learn, how they retain knowledge, and how they apply that knowledge in real-world scenarios.

The Learner Experience Design Graduate Certificate is made up of five courses, all focused on getting you the skills you need to be successful in the field.

Courses

Foundations of Learner Experience Design (4 credits)

This course is an introduction to the topic, and teaches concepts and techniques from design principles, instructional design, user experience design, and learning and development.

User Experience and Design Thinking (4 credits)

This course focuses on user experience, with a user-centered approach. You’ll learn how to find what your learners need and want and test design solutions.

Tools for Learner Experience Design (2 credits)

This course focuses on the eLearning tools you will actually use in your career. You will use these tools to create real learning experiences to meet the needs of an intended audience.

Strategies for Learner Experience Design (2 credits)

This course is about the strategies and tools you will use to promote learner retention. It covers several strategies, all with an underlying emphasis on personalization and design.

Elective (4 credits)

Your academic advisor will help you find which elective is best for you. Recommended classes will help support and elevate your knowledge, with topics including editing, project management, technical communication, and more.

LXD and Your Life

Life doesn’t stop just because you’re in school. Adding school on top of work, family, and personal commitments can be a big ask.

Program Format

Online/Remote

Why That Matters

The LXD program is offered entirely online and asynchronously. That means that you can do coursework when it makes sense for you. It doesn’t matter if you do before the sun comes up, after it goes down, or anywhere in between. It also means that you’re not spending time driving to campus; your campus is anywhere you have an internet connection.

Program Length

5 courses, 16 credit hours

Why That Matters

The certificate is made up of 5 courses over 16 credit hours. Many finish the program in 2-3 semesters (12-18 months). Your academic advisor can help you find the timetable that works best for you and your life.

Graduate courses at Metro State University are $855 per credit hour.*

At 16 credit hours, the total cost of the LXD graduate certificate is $13,680.

*As of 2026, not including any additional fees or expenses.

Age and Adult Learners

One of the biggest challenges when working adults return to college is the concern that they’ll be the oldest person in the room. In Learner Experience Design, that doesn’t have to be part of the equation. The majority of LXD students are adults who have been in the workforce and are now seeking to advance, shift, or re-focus their careers.

Career Outcomes

Learner Experience Design is a field that crosses industries. From mobile apps to non-profit organizations, most industries implement training programs in some form, whether it’s training students, employees, or customers.

Graduates of the Learner Experience Design program are prepared for careers such as:

  • Instructional Designer
  • Learning Architect
  • eLearning Developer
  • Digital Learning Designer
  • Learning Experience Designer
  • Training Design Specialist
